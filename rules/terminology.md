# Terminology

Use standard Australian AEC terminology in all skill outputs. **Default jurisdiction: Western Australia.** This improves consistency and ensures outputs read as professional documents prepared by an Australian practice.

## Spelling

- Use **Australian English** spelling throughout: *colour, centre, organise, programme, metre, neighbour, defence, modelling, traveller, catalogue, storey, kerb*
- Never use US spellings (color, center, organize, program-as-document, meter-as-distance, etc.) unless quoting a US source verbatim
- "Storey" for a level of a building; "Story" only for a narrative

## WA Planning & Development Vocabulary

Define on first use, then abbreviate:

- **GFA** — Gross Floor Area
- **NLA** — Net Lettable Area
- **Plot Ratio** — ratio of GFA to site area (NOT "FAR"; FAR is a US term)
- **R-Code** — residential density code (e.g., R20, R40, R-AC3) under SPP 7.3
- **LPS** — Local Planning Scheme
- **LPP** — Local Planning Policy
- **MRS** — Metropolitan Region Scheme
- **DA** — Development Application
- **DAP** — Development Assessment Panel (JDAP / LDAP)
- **WAPC** — Western Australian Planning Commission
- **DPLH** — Department of Planning, Lands and Heritage
- **SAT** — State Administrative Tribunal (planning appeals)
- **Discretionary use** — a use that may be approved at the discretion of the local government, subject to advertising (cf. "P" permitted, "D" discretionary, "A" advertised, "X" not permitted in LPS use tables)
- **Deep soil zone** — area of unsealed ground supporting deep root growth, per R-Codes Vol. 2
- **Communal open space** / **Private open space** — R-Codes terminology for outdoor amenity
- **Setback** — always specify *primary street, secondary street, side, or rear*
- **Podium / tower** — standard mixed-use form vocabulary; podium is the lower bulk addressing the street, tower the slimmer element above
- **Activation** / **passive surveillance** — standard urban design terms in WA design review and DA assessments

## Heritage Vocabulary (WA)

- **State Registered Place** — listed on the State Register of Heritage Places under the *Heritage Act 2018* (WA)
- **Local Heritage Survey (LHS)** / **Municipal Inventory (MI)** — local government heritage register (LHS is the current name post-2018)
- **Heritage Area** — designated area within an LPS (not "historic district")
- **Conservation Plan** / **Statement of Significance** / **Heritage Impact Statement (HIS)** — standard heritage document types
- Cite the *Burra Charter* (Australia ICOMOS) when discussing conservation principles, not the US Secretary of the Interior's Standards

## Abbreviation Rules

- **Define on first use**, then abbreviate throughout: "Gross Floor Area (GFA)" → "GFA"
- **Never assume** the reader knows an abbreviation — even common ones like HVAC get defined once
- **Domain-specific abbreviations** (GFA, NLA, BAL, JDAP) must always be defined on first use

## Material Names (Australian conventions)

- Use industry-standard Australian names: "plasterboard" not "drywall"; "compressed fibre cement (CFC) sheet" not "Hardiebacker"; "render" not "stucco"
- For products: use the generic material name, not brand names (unless specifying a sole-source product)
- Capitalise proprietary names: "Corian®", "Trespa®", "Colorbond®", "Weathertex®" — lowercase generic equivalents: "solid surface", "HPL panel", "prefinished steel sheet", "hardboard cladding"
- Note: in WA, **Colorbond** is the dominant prefinished steel sheet; **rammed earth, limestone, and jarrah** are regionally significant materials worth naming specifically when relevant

## Numeric Style

- Spell out one through nine in prose; use numerals for 10 and above
- Always use numerals in tables, dimensions, and technical data
- Use numerals with units: "4 m²", "6 m", "3 storeys"
- Dates: Australian format — `21 April 2026` or `21/04/2026` (never US `04/21/2026`)
