# Professional Disclaimer

These rules govern how skill outputs are framed relative to professional practice. **Default jurisdiction: Western Australia.**

## Required Disclaimer

Every skill output that includes code analysis, planning interpretations, structural assumptions, or life-safety calculations must end with:

> **Disclaimer:** This is an AI-generated analysis for preliminary planning purposes only. All findings must be verified by a registered architect, engineer, or other suitably qualified practitioner before use in design development, development applications, building permit submissions, or any other statutory submission. This output is not a substitute for professional advice, certified drawings, or engineering certification.

## Language Rules

- **Never say** "this complies with the NCC / R-Codes / LPS" — say "this appears consistent with NCC 2022 Vol. 1 [clause] / R-Codes Vol. 1 cl. [x] / [LGA] LPS [n] cl. [x]"
- **Never say** "this is code-compliant" — say "based on [cited clause], this appears to meet the deemed-to-satisfy requirement, subject to [BAL assessment / engineering certification / heritage assessment / etc.]"
- **Never say** "approved" or "will be approved" — planning and building approval is a discretionary act of the Responsible Authority; say "appears to satisfy the assessment criteria of [cited instrument]" or "would be expected to be supportable, subject to [factors]"
- **Never say** "no breaches" or "fully compliant" — say "no inconsistencies were identified in the data reviewed"
- **Never present** AI analysis as a substitute for drawings prepared and signed by a registered architect (under the *Architects Act 2004* WA), engineering certification, or assessments by other qualified consultants (heritage, bushfire, acoustic, traffic, etc.)
- **Never claim** completeness — say "based on the sources reviewed" or "from the data available at [date]"
- **Always flag** when WA variations to the NCC may apply, when an LPP may override a deemed provision, or when discretionary planning judgement would determine the outcome

## When to Include

Include the disclaimer on:
- Plot ratio, height, setback, and other planning envelope calculations
- Occupancy load and egress calculations (NCC Vol. 1 Section D / Vol. 2 Part H)
- NCC, R-Codes, or LPS compliance checks
- Structural or building services assumptions
- Environmental risk assessments (flood/storm surge, bushfire/BAL, acid sulfate soils, contaminated sites, coastal erosion / SPP 2.6)
- Heritage impact commentary (State Register, Local Heritage Survey, Heritage Areas)
- Any output the user might submit to a client, local government, JDAP, WAPC, or SAT

Omit the disclaimer on:
- Product research and FF&E schedules (informational, not regulatory)
- Presentation decks (aesthetic output)
- Colour palettes
- Data formatting and conversion tasks (CSV, SIF)
- Internal studio templates and brief templates
