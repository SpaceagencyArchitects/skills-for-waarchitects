## 0911 Plasterboard linings

### 1 General

**1.1 Cross-references**

- `0981 Painting`: paint finish to plasterboard surfaces.
- `0751 Waterproofing — wet areas`: substrate preparation and interface in wet areas.
- `0951 Suspended ceilings`: coordination at transitions between plasterboard bulkheads and lay-in ceilings.
- `2011 Mechanical services` and `2611 Electrical services`: coordination of services penetrations prior to sheet fixing.

**1.2 Standards**

- AS/NZS 2588:2018, *Gypsum plasterboard*.
- AS/NZS 2589.1:2017, *Gypsum linings — Application and finishing, Part 1: General requirements*.
- AS 1530.1:1994, *Methods for fire tests on building materials, components and structures — Combustibility test for materials*.
- AS 1530.4:2014, *Methods for fire tests on building materials, components and structures — Fire-resistance tests for elements of construction*.
- NCC 2022 Vol. 1 Spec C1.1, *Fire-resisting construction*: FRLs for applicable elements.

**1.3 Interpretation**

- FRL: Fire Resistance Level, as defined in NCC 2022 Vol. 1 Schedule 3.
- Finish level: surface finish level per AS/NZS 2589.1.

**1.4 Submissions**

- Product data for plasterboard, jointing compound, tape, steel framing, fasteners, and acoustic sealant.
- Fire-rated system documentation: manufacturer's tested system (identifying FRL, test reference, and sheet layout) for each fire-rated partition and ceiling. **[REVIEW REQUIRED]** Confirm required FRLs and tested system identifiers with the Project Architect, building surveyor, and fire engineer prior to procurement.
- Acoustic-rated system documentation (Rw / Rw + Ctr values) where partitions are required to achieve a specified acoustic performance.

**1.5 Inspections**

- **Witness point:** Stud framing plumb, level, true to line prior to sheet fixing.
- **Hold point:** First-coat jointing complete and inspected before finish coats.

**1.6 Quality**

- Installer to have minimum three years' documented experience with plasterboard installations of comparable scope, and membership of an industry body such as the Association of Wall and Ceiling Industries Australia (AWCI).
- Fire-rated systems to be installed strictly in accordance with the listed tested system without substitution of components.

**1.7 Warranties**

- Installer: minimum 12-month workmanship warranty covering cracking, nail-popping, joint ridging, and other workmanship defects.

### 2 Products

**2.1 Manufacturers**

- CSR Gyprock.
- Knauf Plasterboard.
- USG Boral.
- Or approved equivalent.

**2.2 Materials**

- Standard plasterboard: 13 mm thick, to AS/NZS 2588, non-fire-rated areas.
- Fire-rated plasterboard: 13 mm or 16 mm as required by the tested system, to AS/NZS 2588, for FRL-rated partitions and ceilings.
- Water-resistant plasterboard: to AS/NZS 2588, to walls behind wet-area tiling (refer to `0751 Waterproofing — wet areas`).
- Steel wall framing: minimum 0.50 BMT galvanised steel studs at 600 mm centres maximum, or as required by the tested fire-rated or acoustic system.
- Jointing compound: to AS/NZS 2589.1, proprietary ready-mixed and setting types as appropriate to coat and location.
- Joint tape: paper tape, to AS/NZS 2589.1.
- Acoustic sealant: non-hardening, non-skinning, to perimeters of acoustic-rated and fire-rated partitions.

**2.3 Performance**

- **Surface burning characteristics:** non-combustible to AS 1530.1 where required by NCC 2022 Vol. 1.
- **FRLs:** as indicated on the drawings. Minimum 60/60/60 at corridor walls and tenancy demising walls unless otherwise indicated.
- **Airborne sound insulation (Rw):** Rw 45 minimum at standard office partitions; Rw 50 minimum at meeting rooms and confidential workspaces. **[REVIEW REQUIRED]** Verify against the acoustic consultant's report and NCC 2022 Vol. 1 Part F5 where applicable.

**2.4 Finishes**

- Finish level to AS/NZS 2589.1: Level 4 to surfaces receiving matt or low-sheen paint; Level 5 to surfaces receiving gloss / semi-gloss paint or subject to critical lighting conditions.

**2.5 Components**

- External corner bead: PVC or paper-faced metal corner bead.
- Control joints: at intervals not exceeding 7.2 m in each direction, and at construction and expansion joints in the structure.
- Fasteners: self-drilling, self-tapping screws of type and length recommended by the plasterboard manufacturer for the assembly.

### 3 Execution

**3.1 Preparation**

- Verify structural framing and services rough-in by other trades is complete and inspected.
- Verify the building is enclosed and ambient temperature is maintained above 10 °C during installation.
- Coordinate stud placement to support all wall-mounted fixtures, joinery, and equipment.

**3.2 Installation**

- Install to AS/NZS 2589.1 and the plasterboard manufacturer's written instructions.
- Orient sheets with the long dimension perpendicular to framing members at ceilings.
- Stagger joints on opposite sides of partitions; do not align joints with corners of openings.
- Apply joint tape and compound in the number of coats required to achieve the specified finish level; sand smooth between coats.

**3.3 Tolerances**

- Finished surface flatness: maximum 3 mm deviation under a 1.8 m straightedge in any direction.
- Plumb: ±3 mm in any 2.4 m storey height.

**3.4 Completion**

- Inspect finished surfaces under strong side-lighting to verify finish level and absence of defects.
- Verify fire-rated assemblies conform to the tested system: correct board type, number of layers, fastener spacing, and joint treatment.

**3.5 Cleaning**

- Remove dust and debris from finished surfaces before painting.
- Protect from damage by other trades; rectify damage to achieve specified finish level prior to Practical Completion.

## 0931 Tiling

### 1 General

**1.1 Cross-references**

- `0751 Waterproofing — wet areas`: waterproofing membrane to substrate prior to tiling in wet areas.
- `0911 Plasterboard linings`: substrate preparation for wall tiling.
- `2211 Hydraulic services`: coordination of floor wastes and fixture rough-ins in tiled areas.

**1.2 Standards**

- AS 3958.1:2007, *Ceramic tiles — Guide to the installation of ceramic tiles*.
- AS 3958.2:2008, *Ceramic tiles — Guide to the selection of a ceramic tiling system*.
- AS ISO 13006:2014, *Ceramic tiles — Definitions, classification, characteristics and marking*.
- AS 4586:2013, *Slip resistance classification of new pedestrian surface materials*.
- AS 4992.1:2006, *Ceramic tiles — Slip resistance tests — Wet pendulum*.
- NCC 2022 Vol. 1 Part F1, *Damp and weatherproofing* (for external tiling).

**1.3 Interpretation**

- R-rating: wet pendulum slip resistance classification per AS 4586.
- BOT: barefoot / wet-area slip classification per AS 4586.

**1.4 Submissions**

- Product data for each tile type, adhesive, grout, and waterproofing membrane.
- Samples: two full-size tiles of each type, colour, and finish for Architect approval.
- Shop drawings showing tile layout, pattern, joint widths, setting-out, and transition details.
- Slip resistance test reports per AS 4586 / AS 4992.1 for floor tiles.
- Installer qualifications.

**1.5 Inspections**

- **Hold point:** Waterproofing membrane inspection and testing complete prior to tiling in wet areas.
- **Witness point:** Setting-out and first row laid prior to full field installation.

**1.6 Quality**

- Installer to have minimum five years' documented experience with tiling installations of comparable scope, and to hold a Registered Builder's or Tiler's Licence as required in WA.
- **Mock-up:** Provide a 1200 × 1200 mm mock-up panel on the actual substrate, including tile, grout joints, and a transition to adjacent flooring. Obtain Architect approval before proceeding with production work.

**1.7 Warranties**

- Manufacturer: minimum 10-year product warranty against manufacturing defects.
- Installer: minimum 24-month workmanship warranty against debonding, cracking, and grout failure.

### 2 Products

**2.1 Manufacturers**

- Beaumont Tiles.
- National Tiles.
- Academy Tiles.
- Or approved equivalent.

**2.2 Materials**

- Porcelain tile: to AS ISO 13006, Group BIa (impervious), water absorption ≤ 0.5 % per AS ISO 10545.3.
- Nominal size: 600 × 600 mm, 10 mm thick, rectified edges.
- Adhesive: proprietary polymer-modified cementitious adhesive, to AS ISO 13007, Class C2TE S1 (deformable), suitable for porcelain.
- Grout: proprietary polymer-modified cementitious grout, to AS ISO 13007; colour to be selected by the Architect.
- Waterproofing membrane in wet areas: refer to worksection `0751 Waterproofing — wet areas`.

**2.3 Performance**

- **Slip resistance (dry internal floor tiles):** R9 minimum per AS 4586.
- **Slip resistance (wet-area floor tiles):** P3 (wet pendulum) minimum per AS 4586, or as recommended by the hazard assessment in HB 198:2014.
- **Slip resistance (external / covered external):** P4 minimum per AS 4586.
- **Breaking strength:** ≥ 1300 N to AS ISO 10545.4.
- **Frost resistance (external applications):** comply with AS ISO 10545.12.

**2.4 Finishes**

- Tile colour, pattern, and surface finish: to be selected by the Architect from the manufacturer's standard range.
- Surface finish: matt or natural texture; polished finishes shall not be used in wet areas or external locations.

**2.5 Components**

- Tile trims: matching porcelain bullnose or proprietary aluminium edge trim as indicated on the drawings.
- Movement-joint sealant: refer to `0731 Membranes` — silicone sealant colour-matched to grout.

### 3 Execution

**3.1 Preparation**

- Verify substrates are sound, clean, dry, and free of curing compounds and contaminants.
- Concrete substrates: verify minimum 28-day cure and moisture content per adhesive manufacturer's requirements.
- Verify substrate flatness: 3 mm maximum deviation under a 3 m straightedge.

**3.2 Installation**

- Install to AS 3958.1 and the adhesive manufacturer's written instructions.
- Notched-trowel application; for tiles larger than 300 × 300 mm in wet areas or external locations, use the buttering + floating ("back-butter") method and achieve a minimum 90 % adhesive coverage.
- Joint width: 3 mm unless otherwise indicated on the drawings.
- Provide movement joints at the perimeter of tiled areas, at changes in plane, over structural movement joints, and at intervals not exceeding 4.5 m in each direction per AS 3958.1.
- Grout joints after adhesive has cured to the manufacturer's required strength.

**3.3 Tolerances**

- Finished floor surface flatness: 3 mm deviation under a 3 m straightedge, with no lippage greater than 1 mm between adjacent tiles.

**3.4 Completion**

- Verify adhesive coverage by periodic tile lift during the works.
- Test wet-area waterproofing to AS 3740 before tiling (refer to `0751 Waterproofing — wet areas`).

**3.5 Cleaning**

- Clean tile surfaces of excess adhesive and grout within 24 hours.
- Apply grout sealer to cementitious grout joints after full cure.
- Protect finished tiling with heavy kraft paper or non-staining protective covering until Practical Completion.

### 3 Execution

**3.1 Preparation**

- Verify overhead structure is complete and can support suspension loads plus a minimum 0.25 kPa live load allowance for future fittings.
- Verify above-ceiling services rough-in is complete and inspected.

**3.2 Installation**

- Install to AS/NZS 2785 and the manufacturer's written instructions.
- Start set-out from the centre of each room or a prominent sight-line to achieve balanced border tiles; border tiles shall not be less than half a full tile width.
- Install hanger wires plumb, at 1200 mm centres maximum, secured to structural members only. Do not fix hangers to ductwork, piping, or non-structural elements.

**3.3 Tolerances**

- Grid level: ±3 mm deviation in any 3 m.

**3.4 Completion**

- Verify grid is locked, spliced, and clipped as required.
- Verify tiles are free from chips, cracks, stains, and misalignment.

**3.5 Cleaning**

- Replace damaged, soiled, or sagging tiles prior to Practical Completion.
- Clean grid and tile surfaces with a dry cloth or manufacturer-approved cleaner.
- Provide the Principal with minimum 2 % spare tiles (minimum 6 tiles per type), stored in a location designated by the Principal.

- Adhesive: manufacturer's recommended low-VOC flooring adhesive.

### 3 Execution

**3.1 Preparation**

- Verify concrete subfloor moisture content per manufacturer's requirements (generally maximum 75 % RH per ASTM F2170 or equivalent AS method).
- Subfloor flatness: 3 mm under a 3 m straightedge.
- Ambient temperature: 18–27 °C during installation and for 48 hours after.
- Fill depressions with proprietary cementitious underlayment compatible with the specified adhesive.

**3.2 Installation**

- Install to AS 1884 and the manufacturer's written instructions.
- Stagger end joints minimum 300 mm between adjacent rows.
- Maintain 6 mm expansion gap at fixed vertical surfaces for floating installations.
- Roll glue-down installations with a 45 kg three-section roller within the adhesive's open time.

**3.3 Tolerances**

- Finished surface flatness: 3 mm under a 3 m straightedge; no lippage between adjacent planks.

**3.4 Completion**

- Inspect for gaps, lifting edges, pattern misalignment, and damage.
- Verify adhesion by lifting a plank at three random locations per 100 m².

**3.5 Cleaning**

- Clean per manufacturer's instructions.
- Protect with breathable non-staining paper; do not use polyethylene sheeting directly on the flooring.

## 0981 Painting

### 1 General

- `0561 Metalwork`: shop-primed metalwork to receive field paint.

**1.2 Standards**

- AS/NZS 3730 (all parts), *Guide to the properties of paints for buildings*.
- AS 1580 (all parts), *Paints and related materials — Methods of test*.
- APAS (Australian Paint Approval Scheme) specifications where applicable.
- NCC 2022 Vol. 1 Spec C1.10, *Fire hazard properties* — where surface spread of flame is regulated.

**1.3 Submissions**

- Product data for each paint product, including VOC content, coverage rate, and recommended dry film thickness.
- Colour samples: two A4 painted draw-down samples of each colour for Architect approval.
- VOC compliance documentation per Green Star / WELL requirements where applicable (typical commercial benchmark: ≤ 50 g/L for interior walls).

**1.4 Quality**

- Painter to have minimum three years' experience in commercial interior painting in WA.
- All paint products to comply with VOC limits of the Green Star Design & As Built — Indoor Environment Quality credit where the project targets a Green Star rating.

**1.5 Warranties**

- Manufacturer: standard product warranty.
- Installer: minimum 24-month warranty covering peeling, blistering, and excessive fading.

### 2 Products

**2.1 Manufacturers**

- Dulux.
- Haymes Paint.
- Wattyl (Taubmans Professional).
- Or approved equivalent.

**2.2 Materials**

- Primer to plasterboard: interior acrylic primer-sealer, low-VOC.
- Finish coat to plasterboard walls: interior acrylic low-sheen, washable, to AS/NZS 3730.
- Finish coat to plasterboard ceilings: interior acrylic matt ceiling paint.
- Primer to ferrous metal: proprietary anti-corrosive DTM (direct-to-metal) primer.
- Finish coat to metalwork: acrylic semi-gloss enamel or DTM enamel.

**2.3 Performance**

- **VOC content:** ≤ 16 g/L for interior low-sheen acrylic (Green Star target); ≤ 50 g/L for semi-gloss enamel.
- **Scrub resistance (walls):** minimum 1000 cycles per AS 1580.455.2.
- **Hide / opacity:** manufacturer's published coverage rate achieved in two finish coats.
- **Group number where applicable:** where the coating is required to satisfy NCC Spec C1.10, provide a coating tested to AS 5637.1.

**2.4 Finishes**

- Colours: as selected by the Architect. Allow for a maximum of six colours across the project unless otherwise noted.
- Sheen: matt at ceilings; low-sheen at walls; semi-gloss at door frames, metalwork, and wet-area walls.

**2.5 Components**

- Caulk: paintable acrylic sealant to minor gaps at trims, skirtings, and frame perimeters.
- Patching compound: non-shrinking acrylic filler for minor surface repairs prior to priming.

### 3 Execution

**3.1 Preparation**

- Verify plasterboard finish level is complete and accepted.
- Verify substrates are dry, clean, and free from dust, grease, and efflorescence.
- Do not commence painting until ambient temperature is between 10 °C and 35 °C and relative humidity is below 85 %.
- Mask and protect adjacent surfaces, flooring, joinery, and fittings not scheduled for paint.
- Fill imperfections, caulk gaps, and sand smooth before priming.

**3.2 Installation**

- Apply to AS/NZS 2311 and the manufacturer's written instructions.
- One coat of primer and two finish coats unless otherwise indicated on the drawings.
- Apply by roller with brush cutting-in, or by airless spray with back-rolling as appropriate.
- Allow manufacturer's specified recoat time between coats.
- Maintain a wet edge to prevent lap marks; achieve uniform colour and sheen free of runs, sags, brush marks, and holidays.

**3.3 Tolerances**

- Dry film thickness to meet manufacturer's published minimum.
- Uniform finish indistinguishable from adjacent work in normal and side-lit conditions.

**3.4 Completion**

- Inspect under normal and side-lit conditions.
- Touch up damaged areas to match adjacent work.

**3.5 Cleaning**

- Remove masking tape and protective coverings promptly after the final coat is touch-dry.
- Clean paint spatter from adjacent surfaces immediately.
- Provide the Principal with 4 L of each colour for future touch-ups, labelled with colour name, formula, product name, and location of use.
