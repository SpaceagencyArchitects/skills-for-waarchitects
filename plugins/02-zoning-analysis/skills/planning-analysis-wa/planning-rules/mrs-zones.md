# MRS & Regional Scheme Zones

Reference for the Metropolitan Region Scheme (MRS) and the two other WA regional schemes. State-level zones and reservations set by the WAPC — they sit above the LPS layer.

## Metropolitan Region Scheme (MRS) — Perth metropolitan area

Covers all 30 LGAs in the Perth metropolitan area, from Two Rocks in the north to Singleton in the south, inland to the Darling Scarp.

### Zones — where development is generally permitted under the relevant LPS

| MRS zone | Typical LPS character | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Urban | Residential, mixed use, commercial | The default developable zone; LPS zoning determines specific use |
| Urban Deferred | Reserved for future Urban rezoning | Minimal development permitted until reclassified |
| Industrial | Manufacturing, warehousing, logistics | LPS zoning may further define Light / General / Strategic Industry |
| Central City | Perth CBD | Under *City of Perth Act 2016*; City of Perth LPS26 applies |
| Rural | Agricultural, rural living | Low-density; LPS may allow tourism, extractive, etc. |

### Reservations — land reserved for public purposes (generally not developable as-of-right)

| MRS reservation | What's reserved for | Key implications |
|---|---|---|
| Parks and Recreation | Public open space, regional parks | No private development; Land acquisition process may apply |
| Public Purposes | Hospitals, schools, civic buildings | Use restricted to the specified public purpose |
| Primary Regional Roads | Major state roads | Main Roads WA controls; setback and access restrictions |
| Other Regional Roads | Secondary state roads | Main Roads WA referral |
| Railways | Rail corridors | PTA / ARC Infrastructure; no building over rail reserves |
| Waterways | Rivers, estuaries | DBCA / DPLH; usually no development |
| State Forests | State forest estate | DBCA |

**If your site is in a Reservation**, development is generally not permitted as-of-right. The Responsible Authority is usually the WAPC (not the LGA), and a separate process applies. Check with the LGA and the WAPC early.

### Special Control Areas

Some MRS reservations overlay additional controls via Special Control Areas (SCA). These vary — check the specific LPS for any declared SCAs over your site.

## Peel Region Scheme (PRS)

Covers the Peel region south of Perth — Mandurah, Murray, Waroona, Boddington. Same structure as MRS: zones (Urban, Urban Deferred, Rural, Industrial, Central City) and reservations (Parks and Recreation, Railways, etc.).

## Greater Bunbury Region Scheme (GBRS)

Covers the Greater Bunbury region — Bunbury, Harvey, Capel, Dardanup. Same structure as MRS.

## How to read the MRS zone for a site

1. Open [WAPC MyPlan WA](https://app-ll.planning.wa.gov.au/MyPlan/)
2. Search for the address
3. Zoom in until the MRS layer displays colour
4. Match the colour / label to the legend
5. Note the zone name exactly as shown — `Urban`, `Urban Deferred`, `Parks and Recreation`, etc.

## How the MRS interacts with the LPS

- The MRS zone is the **envelope** — the LPS zoning must be broadly consistent with the MRS
- An LPS cannot zone land for Urban residential if the MRS has it as Parks and Recreation
- Amendments to the MRS are rare and slow (typically 2–5 years) and require Minister for Planning approval
- Amendments to the LPS are more common but still take 6–18 months

## Non-metro WA — outside regional scheme areas

Outside the Perth, Peel, and Greater Bunbury regions, there is no regional scheme. The LPS for the local LGA is the only zoning instrument, and the WAPC's role is limited to subdivision, MRS-equivalent strategies, and SPPs.

Examples of non-metro LGAs: Shire of Augusta-Margaret River, City of Albany, City of Kalgoorlie-Boulder, Shire of Broome. Each has its own LPS but no overlaying regional scheme.
