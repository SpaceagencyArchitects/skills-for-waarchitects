# R-Codes Vol. 1 — Single Houses & Grouped Dwellings

Reference for State Planning Policy 7.3, Residential Design Codes Vol. 1. Applies to single houses and grouped dwellings at codes R5 through R100+.

**This is a reference summary for preliminary analysis, not a substitute for the full R-Codes PDF.** Always verify against the current version at [WAPC R-Codes](https://www.wa.gov.au/government/publications/state-planning-policy-73-residential-design-codes).

## When Vol. 1 applies

- **Single House** on a lot
- **Ancillary Dwelling** on a lot with a Single House
- **Grouped Dwellings** (strata-titled, attached or detached, usually up to 3 storeys)
- **Multiple Dwellings at R5–R25 or < 4 storeys** (Vol. 2 takes over otherwise)

## Density table (site area per dwelling, m²)

The R-Code defines three site area figures — minimum, average, and maximum — per dwelling. The **average** is usually the binding constraint for yield calculations on grouped dwelling sites.

| R-Code | Min site area per dwelling (m²) | Average (m²) | Max (m²) |
|---|---|---|---|
| R5 | 1600 | 2000 | — |
| R10 | 800 | 1000 | — |
| R12.5 | 700 | 800 | — |
| R15 | 600 | 666 | — |
| R17.5 | 500 | 571 | — |
| R20 | 350 | 450 | 500 |
| R25 | 300 | 350 | 450 |
| R30 | 260 | 300 | 350 |
| R35 | 220 | 260 | 300 |
| R40 | 180 | 220 | 260 |
| R50 | 160 | 180 | 220 |
| R60 | 150 | 166 | 200 |
| R80 | 120 | 125 | 160 |
| R100 | 90 | 100 | 120 |

**Formula:**
- `Max dwellings = site area ÷ average site area per dwelling` (round down)
- For the site area to *qualify* for any dwelling count, **every** proposed lot must be ≥ minimum; collectively they must average ≥ average. Maximum applies in certain codes as the largest any individual lot can be.

**Example:** R40 site of 880 m². Average per dwelling = 220 m². Max dwellings = 880 ÷ 220 = 4. All 4 lots must be between 180 m² and 260 m² each, averaging ≥ 220 m² overall.

Primary street setback varies by R-Code. For most R-Codes:

| R-Code | Primary street setback (DTS) |
|---|---|
| R5 | 10 m average, 7.5 m minimum |
| R10 | 7.5 m average, 6 m minimum |
| R12.5 | 6 m average, 4 m minimum |
| R15 | 6 m |
| R17.5–R20 | 6 m |
| R25 | 4 m |
| R30 | 4 m |
| R35 | 4 m |
| R40 | 4 m |
| R50 | 4 m |
| R60 | 2 m (Vol. 1) |
| R80 | 2 m (Vol. 1) |
| R100 | 2 m (Vol. 1) |

**Note:** These are DTS (deemed-to-satisfy). LPS setback overrides are common and frequently more restrictive (particularly in heritage areas and on primary streets).

## Setbacks — side and rear

**Simplified Vol. 1 side-setback table** (walls up to 3 m average / 3.5 m maximum height, no major openings):

| R-Code | Side setback to boundary |
|---|---|
| R20 | 1.0 m |
| R30 | 1.0 m |
| R40 | 1.0 m |
| R60 | 1.0 m |
| R80–R100 | 1.0 m |

If the wall has **major openings** (windows to habitable rooms), setback increases (commonly to 1.5–3.0 m depending on wall length and R-Code).

If the wall is **taller** (> 3.5 m) or **longer** (> 9 m), setbacks increase further — consult the full Vol. 1 setback table (Table 2a / 2b in Vol. 1).

**Rear setback:**
- Generally follows the same principles as side setbacks
- For lots > 300 m² and R40 or below: often 6 m minimum to allow rear outdoor living

**Boundary walls** are permitted up to a specified length and height under each R-Code (typically up to 9 m long and 3 m high for R20–R40, with variations). This is a DTS provision — abutting boundary walls must meet the R-Code table or rely on design principles.

## Building height (wall height, not Vol. 2 "building height")

Vol. 1 controls external wall height rather than overall building height:

| R-Code | Max external wall height (pitched roof) | Max external wall height (flat/concealed roof) |
|---|---|---|
| R5–R20 | 6.0 m | 7.0 m |
| R25–R40 | 6.0 m | 7.0 m |
| R60 | 6.0 m | 7.0 m |
| R80+ | 9.0 m (Vol. 1 generally doesn't apply at these codes with multi-storey — Vol. 2 takes over) | — |

Plus a **pitched roof allowance** — usually up to 9 m to the highest roof ridge, with roof pitch between 10° and 36°.

**LPS height limits** are extremely common and often more restrictive — 6 m, 7 m, or 2 storeys are typical limits in character-sensitive areas (e.g. Fremantle West End, Subiaco Heritage Area). Always apply the more restrictive limit.

## Private open space per dwelling

Each dwelling must have a contiguous outdoor living area:

| R-Code | Minimum POS per dwelling (m²) | Minimum dimension |
|---|---|---|
| R5–R25 | 30 m² | 4 m |
| R30–R40 | 24 m² | 4 m |
| R50–R60 (Vol. 1) | 16 m² | 3 m |
| R80+ | See Vol. 2 balcony provisions | — |

## Car parking

| Dwelling type | Bays required (DTS) |
|---|---|
| Single House | 2 bays (R40 and below) / 1 bay (R50+) |
| Grouped Dwelling — 1 bedroom | 1 bay |
| Grouped Dwelling — 2 bedroom | 1 bay + 0.25 visitor per dwelling |
| Grouped Dwelling — 3+ bedroom | 2 bays + 0.25 visitor per dwelling |
| Ancillary Dwelling | 0 additional (shares primary) |

Visitor bays: typically 0.25 per dwelling rounded up, in addition to resident bays.

LPS parking rates for non-residential uses (shops, offices) sit separately from R-Codes — cite the LPS parking schedule.

## Design principles — the performance-based path

Every DTS provision has a corresponding **design principle**. If a proposal can't meet the DTS (for whatever reason), it can instead demonstrate compliance with the design principle — but this requires the exercise of discretion by the Responsible Authority and good design argument.

Typical design principles cover:
- Streetscape amenity
- Privacy and overlooking
- Solar access
- Outdoor living
- Parking and access
- Bulk and scale

A proposal relying on design principles is **not** as-of-right — it's a discretionary decision. Budget for design review, negotiation, and possible advertising.

## Common Vol. 1 pitfalls

- **Boundary wall length** — check the cumulative length across all boundary walls, not just single walls
- **Overshadowing** — Vol. 1 has a winter overshadowing test for adjoining lots; often the binding constraint on 2-storey additions
- **Visual privacy** — cone-of-vision tests for habitable room windows over 4 m from natural ground level
- **Outdoor living retreat** — must be contiguous, min 4 m width, usable
- **Solar access** — passive design principles are DTS for most codes; retrofit site analysis at DA stage
