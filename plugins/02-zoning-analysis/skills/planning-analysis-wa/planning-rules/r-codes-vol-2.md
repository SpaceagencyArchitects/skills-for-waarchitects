# R-Codes Vol. 2 — Apartments

Reference for State Planning Policy 7.3, Residential Design Codes Vol. 2 (Apartments). Applies to multiple dwellings in apartment form — typically at R30+ with ≥ 4 storeys, or at any of the activity-centre codes (R-AC0, R-AC1, R-AC2, R-AC3).

**This is a reference summary for preliminary analysis, not a substitute for the full R-Codes PDF.** Always verify against the current version at [WAPC R-Codes](https://www.wa.gov.au/government/publications/state-planning-policy-73-residential-design-codes).

## When Vol. 2 applies

- **Multiple Dwelling** development at R30 or higher, typically 4+ storeys
- **Development at activity-centre codes**: R-AC0, R-AC1, R-AC2, R-AC3
- **Mixed Use** with apartment component

If both a Vol. 1 code (e.g. R60) and a Vol. 2 code (e.g. R-AC3) apply to the site, the **applicant can choose** which pathway to pursue. Vol. 2 typically yields more dwellings but requires meeting the full apartment design controls.

## Density codes — plot ratio

Vol. 2 uses **plot ratio** (GFA ÷ site area) instead of site area per dwelling.

| R-Code | Plot ratio (DTS) | Typical max storeys |
|---|---|---|
| R30 | 0.5 | 4 |
| R40 | 0.6 | 4 |
| R60 | 0.7 | 5 |
| R80 | 0.8 | 6 |
| R100 | 1.0 | 6 |
| R160 | 1.25 | 8 |
| R-AC0 | 0.75 | Negotiable / per LPS |
| R-AC1 | 1.0 | Negotiable / per LPS |
| R-AC2 | 1.25 | Negotiable / per LPS |
| R-AC3 | 1.5 | Negotiable / per LPS |

**Note:** Activity Centre codes (R-AC series) defer height / storey limits to the LPS or activity centre plan. The plot ratio is the control; the LPS sets the height.

**What counts as plot ratio area (GFA):**
- All habitable and commercial floor area
- Excludes: lift shafts, stairwells, lobbies for vertical circulation, balconies (subject to limits), plant, basement parking, storage, communal facilities
- The exact inclusion / exclusion list varies — consult R-Codes Vol. 2 cl. [Plot Ratio Area] for the current definition

## Building height

Vol. 2 height is usually expressed in **storeys** + **metres**. The binding control varies by LPS:

- **If the LPS sets a height (in metres or storeys)**: use the LPS figure
- **If the LPS references the R-Codes**: use the Vol. 2 table storey count + typical floor-to-floor assumption (3.3 m commercial, 3.0 m residential)

**Typical apartment building height** (DTS):

| R-Code | Storeys | Approx. max height (m) |
|---|---|---|
| R30 | 4 | 14 |
| R40 | 4 | 14 |
| R60 | 5 | 17 |
| R80 | 6 | 20 |
| R100 | 6 | 20 |
| R-AC3 | Per LPS (commonly 4–8) | Per LPS |

## Setbacks — apartments

Vol. 2 setbacks are more nuanced than Vol. 1 and depend on:
- Whether the boundary is **primary street**, **secondary street**, **side**, or **rear**
- The **height** of the wall at that point
- Whether the wall has **major openings** (habitable room windows)
- The adjacent land use (e.g. apartment vs. low-rise residential vs. commercial)

**Simplified primary street setback** (for apartments):

| R-Code | Typical primary street setback (DTS) |
|---|---|
| R30 | 4 m |
| R40 | 3–4 m |
| R60 | 2–3 m |
| R80+ | 2 m — or zero in mixed-use / activity centre context |
| R-AC1–3 | Usually zero — podium built to street boundary |

**Side and rear setbacks** — graduated by height (`nil` setback at ground, increasing with height):

| Wall height | Typical side setback |
|---|---|
| Up to 3 m | 1.5 m |
| 3–6 m | 2–3 m |
| 6–12 m | 3–6 m |
| 12 m+ | 6 m+, graduated |

**Tower setbacks** above a podium: usually 3–6 m from the street edge and 6 m+ from side boundaries, to preserve street proportions and separation between towers.

Always cross-check the LPS — activity centre plans often impose **podium / tower envelope diagrams** that override the R-Codes DTS.

## Open space — Vol. 2

Apartments don't have a "% of site" open space requirement like Vol. 1. Instead, the controls are:

### Communal open space (COS)
- Required on all developments of 5+ dwellings
- Minimum area: usually **5–10%** of site area (varies by R-Code; check Vol. 2)
- Must meet size, dimension, solar access, and usability criteria
- Can be at ground, podium, or roof

### Private open space (balconies / courtyards)
| Dwelling type | Minimum POS | Minimum dimension |
|---|---|---|
| Studio / 1-bed | 8 m² | 2 m |
| 2-bed | 10 m² | 2 m |
| 3+ bed | 12 m² | 2.4 m |
| Ground-floor dwelling | 16 m² courtyard | 4 m |

### Deep soil zones
Vol. 2 requires a percentage of the site to be **deep soil** — unsealed ground, capable of supporting medium / large trees, minimum dimensions for root growth.

| Site area | Minimum deep soil % |
|---|---|
| < 1,500 m² | 7% |
| 1,500–10,000 m² | 10% |
| > 10,000 m² | 15% |

Deep soil zones must have minimum dimensions (usually 3 m × 3 m for small trees, 6 m × 6 m for large trees) and meet landscape design criteria.

## Dwelling mix

Vol. 2 encourages dwelling mix — avoiding all-studio or all-1-bed buildings:

**Typical target mix (DTS):**
- At least 20% of dwellings must be 2+ bedroom
- No more than 40% can be studio

These are indicative — the DTS varies by project context. The design principle is to provide a range of dwelling types.

## Minimum dwelling sizes

| Dwelling type | Minimum internal area (NSA) |
|---|---|
| Studio | 35 m² |
| 1-bedroom | 50 m² |
| 2-bedroom | 70 m² |
| 3-bedroom | 95 m² |

These exclude balconies and external storage.

## Car parking — apartments

| Dwelling type | Bays required (DTS) |
|---|---|
| Studio / 1-bed | 0.75–1 bay |
| 2-bed | 1 bay |
| 3+ bed | 1.25–1.5 bays |

Plus **visitor parking** at typically 0.25 bays per dwelling.

**Reductions for transit access:** Vol. 2 allows reductions in parking rates where the site is within defined walking distances of a bus / rail station. Check the current Vol. 2 for the exact reduction table.

**Bike parking:** Vol. 2 requires bike parking for residents (typically 1 per dwelling) plus visitor bike bays.

## Other key Vol. 2 provisions

- **Natural ventilation**: 60% of dwellings must have cross-ventilation (habitable rooms on two external walls or via a light well)
- **Solar access**: 70% of dwellings must receive direct sunlight to their main living room for at least 2 hours between 9 AM and 3 PM on the winter solstice (21 June)
- **Visual privacy**: habitable room windows must be set back from facing windows on adjoining lots; cone-of-vision tests apply
- **Noise attenuation**: if the site is within the SPP 5.4 influence area, the acoustic requirements flow through to Vol. 2 façade provisions
- **Wind / microclimate**: larger developments may require a wind study
- **Reflectivity**: façade materials must not create glare (reflectivity index limits)
- **Adaptable dwellings**: minimum percentage of dwellings must meet silver-level adaptable design (Livable Housing Design Guidelines) — typically 20%
- **Storage**: minimum external storage per dwelling (usually 4 m³ for 1-bed, 8 m³ for 3-bed)

## Activity centre (R-AC) codes — special notes

The R-AC series (R-AC0 through R-AC3) are for activity centres — places identified in the WAPC's Activity Centres hierarchy (SPP 4.2) as strategic, district, neighbourhood, or local centres.

For R-AC sites:
- Plot ratio applies as per the table above
- Height, setbacks, streetwall requirements are almost always set by the **LPS or an Activity Centre Structure Plan**, not the R-Codes
- Refer to the LPS cl. relating to the activity centre, or the Structure Plan if declared
- Built form typically: podium (2–4 storeys at street edge) + tower (setback above podium)

## Design principles (performance-based path)

As with Vol. 1, Vol. 2 provides **design principles** alongside every DTS. A scheme that doesn't meet DTS can apply via design principles, subject to discretion.

Design review: schemes of 4+ storeys or 10+ dwellings often require **Design Review Panel** input (or a State Design Review Panel referral for larger / strategic sites) per Design WA policy.
